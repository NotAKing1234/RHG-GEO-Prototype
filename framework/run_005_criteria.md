## C01 - AI Crawler and Index Access
**Signal name:** Public AI/search crawler access for every selected page
**Why the signal matters for AI hotel discovery:** AI systems cannot cite or compare pages they cannot retrieve. In this run the selected workload is 478 Park Plaza URLs, so every URL needs a status record rather than representative sampling.
**What "passing" looks like:** The audited URL returns HTTP 200 useful HTML or an indexable redirect; failures such as 403, AccessDenied, timeout, empty HTML, or blocked templates are recorded precisely.
**How the signal should be checked during the audit:** Fetch every SQLite run_url_targets URL with a normal browser-like user agent; record status, final URL, title, access-template hints, and notes.
**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** A US traveler using AI for European hotel planning will not see Park Plaza facts when the page cannot be retrieved.

## C02 - Locale, Hreflang, and Canonical Clarity
**Signal name:** English/US and European localized pages expose coherent language, canonical, and alternate signals
**Why the signal matters for AI hotel discovery:** The run is Park Plaza-heavy and mostly en-us, so locale mismatch can send US-origin queries to weak or wrong regional pages.
**What "passing" looks like:** HTML lang, canonical, hreflang alternates, title/meta language, and URL locale are consistent with the selected page purpose.
**How the signal should be checked during the audit:** Extract lang, canonical, hreflang, locale in URL, title/meta language, and country/region terms.
**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** American travelers need English/US or clearly relevant European content for Europe trip planning.

## C03 - Hotel/LodgingBusiness Structured Data
**Signal name:** Valid hotel-specific JSON-LD exists on property pages
**Why the signal matters for AI hotel discovery:** Organization and BreadcrumbList schema alone are too shallow for hotel comparisons. Assistants need hotel/place facts.
**What "passing" looks like:** Hotel, LodgingBusiness, or relevant subtype JSON-LD includes name, URL, address, geo, telephone, image, description, amenityFeature, rating/review where valid, and booking/offer route where applicable.
**How the signal should be checked during the audit:** Extract JSON-LD and microdata summaries, normalize @type values, and check hotel-specific fields against visible content.
**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** Bleisure comparison queries rely on amenity, location, rating, and booking facts.

**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** US business travelers often combine meeting needs with nearby leisure or airport/train access.
