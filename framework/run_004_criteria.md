## C01 — AI Crawler and Index Access
**Signal name:** Public AI/search crawler access for audited pages
**Why the signal matters for AI hotel discovery:** Google AI features depend on crawlable indexed pages; ChatGPT Search requires OAI-Searchbot access; agentic travel tools need reachable pages to verify availability and details.
**What "passing" looks like:** The audited URL and domain root return HTTP 200 or indexable redirects for normal browser requests and major retrieval crawlers; `/robots.txt` is reachable; no WAF/access template blocks the page; pages are eligible for snippets/indexing.
**How the signal should be checked during the audit:** Fetch the URL, `/robots.txt`, and representative root paths with a normal browser user agent and record HTTP status, final URL, title, and whether an access-restricted template was returned.
**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** If AI engines cannot retrieve the page, Radisson cannot be cited for US-to-Europe business/leisure hotel prompts.

## C02 — Query Fan-Out Coverage
**Signal name:** Page answers multi-intent travel subqueries generated by AI Mode/query fan-out
**Why the signal matters for AI hotel discovery:** Google AI Mode and AI Overviews may issue related searches across subtopics; a hotel page must cover location, amenities, traveler type, reviews, booking, and use case together.
**What "passing" looks like:** Title, meta description, headings, and body copy cover at least three linked sub-intents such as city/region, hotel type, meeting/work amenities, breakfast/parking, leisure proximity, and booking trust.
**How the signal should be checked during the audit:** Compare title, meta, H1-H3, and first body section against likely fan-out queries for the page's brand/location/use case.
**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** Bleisure searches are compound by nature: destination plus work needs plus leisure needs plus trust/price.

## C03 — Hotel/LodgingBusiness Structured Data
**Signal name:** Valid Hotel or LodgingBusiness JSON-LD with useful hotel facts
**Why the signal matters for AI hotel discovery:** Structured data helps search systems classify and validate hotel entities, amenities, ratings, addresses, offers, and local facts.
**What "passing" looks like:** Page contains valid JSON-LD using `Hotel`, `LodgingBusiness`, or a relevant subtype with visible-page-aligned fields: name, URL, address, geo, telephone, image, description, amenityFeature, aggregateRating/reviews where available, checkInTime/checkOutTime, starRating, and priceRange or offer data when applicable.
**How the signal should be checked during the audit:** Extract JSON-LD blocks, normalize `@type`, and record whether required/recommended hotel facts are present and match visible content.
**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** Amenity, location, rating, and check-in data are exactly the fields assistants need for hotel comparison answers.

## C04 — Local Business and Place Detail Completeness
**Signal name:** Machine-readable local/place details for individual hotel pages
**Why the signal matters for AI hotel discovery:** Local business details feed search panels, maps, local recommendations, and AI answers for "near me" or district-specific hotel queries.
**What "passing" looks like:** Individual hotel pages expose address, locality, country, geo coordinates, telephone, opening/check-in details, reviews, business category, and reservation/booking route; details are consistent with Google Business Profile and major OTAs.
**How the signal should be checked during the audit:** Extract structured data and visible contact/location sections; compare against page title/meta and obvious OTA/brand facts where available.
**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** US travelers planning European trips ask by neighborhood, airport, train station, meeting venue, and city-center access.

## C05 — Visible Q&A and FAQ Integrity
**Signal name:** Real traveler questions answered visibly, with FAQPage only when accurate
**Why the signal matters for AI hotel discovery:** Q&A content helps assistants answer direct questions, but markup must truthfully describe visible content and should not be maintained only for retired Google FAQ rich-result behavior.
**What "passing" looks like:** Page includes visible Q&A for real traveler decisions and, where schema is used, `FAQPage`/`Question`/`Answer` JSON-LD exactly matches visible questions and answers without promotional stuffing.
**How the signal should be checked during the audit:** Search the rendered/HTML text for FAQ/Q&A blocks and extract any FAQPage schema; verify question count, topical relevance, and visible/schema alignment.
**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** Questions such as breakfast, Wi-Fi, meeting rooms, parking, check-in, transport, and weekend extension map directly to bleisure prompts.

## C06 — Factual First-Screen Content
**Signal name:** First visible content is factual and non-commodity, not only brand slogans
**Why the signal matters for AI hotel discovery:** Google guidance favors helpful, reliable, people-first, non-commodity content; AI summaries need extractable facts rather than generic brand language.
**What "passing" looks like:** First 80-120 words include concrete facts: brand/property count, location scope, room or hotel count, key amenities, traveler use case, and named cities/regions where relevant.
**How the signal should be checked during the audit:** Capture first body text from the page or reader fallback and score whether at least three concrete facts appear before promotional copy dominates.
**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** Assistants need concise facts to answer "is this good for my work trip plus weekend stay?"

## C07 — Structured Amenity and Work-Leisure Data
**Signal name:** Meeting, workspace, breakfast, parking, Wi-Fi, wellness, and leisure amenities are scannable
**Why the signal matters for AI hotel discovery:** Hospitality assistants compare properties by amenities and suitability, not by brand adjectives.
**What "passing" looks like:** Amenities appear in schema `amenityFeature`, tables, bullets, or clear sections; business amenities and leisure amenities are both present when relevant; meeting-room capacity and breakfast/parking/Wi-Fi are explicit if offered.
**How the signal should be checked during the audit:** Search JSON-LD and headings/body text for amenity terms; record whether amenities are specific, structured, and close to the primary content.
**Relevance to American bleisure traveler queries:** The target traveler is comparing work requirements with leisure comfort and convenience.
