### GAP-008 | utility/test/picklist URLs in target set | C15
**Best practice for closing it:** Sitemaps and GEO target sets should contain only canonical, indexable, production landing pages. Utility, search, booking-flow, faceted, test, and picklist URLs should be excluded from sitemaps; if public but not index-worthy, use `noindex` on crawlable pages or robots disallow for crawl-waste patterns. Google warns faceted/utility URL spaces can cause overcrawling and slower discovery of useful URLs. Sources: https://developers.google.com/crawling/docs/faceted-navigation, https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/block-indexing, https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/url-structure.
**Competitor or OTA example doing this well:** Hyatt's robots file declares hotel/browse sitemaps while disallowing `/search/`, `/book/`, `/reservation/`, `/quickbook/`, and group-booking utility paths across locales. Evidence: https://www.hyatt.com/robots.txt.
**Specific proposed fix:** Remove `/meeting-conference/picklist`, sitemap-like, and internal utility patterns from `run_url_targets` selection logic and any generated sitemaps. Apply `X-Robots-Tag: noindex` or robots disallow to utility endpoints that must remain public. Replace them with clean property/city meeting landing pages that have canonical URLs and page-specific content.
**Directional impact for bleisure traveler discovery:** Cleaner target sets reduce AI confusion and crawl waste. Assistants should find "Park Plaza Westminster Bridge meetings" or "Park Plaza Berlin offer," not internal picklists that do not answer traveler intent.

## Assessment
**Most urgent for AI engine visibility right now:** GAP-001. Page access is the dependency for every other fix. If 476 of 478 selected URLs return access-restricted templates, schema, hreflang, offers, and meeting content cannot be evaluated or cited.

**Fastest measurable improvement:** Unblock page-specific 200 responses for the selected Park Plaza URLs and make `/sitemap.xml` public. Measure with a recrawl: 200-rate, access-template fingerprint disappearance, unique page titles, extracted schema count, and sitemap fetch success in Google/Bing tooling.

**Contested or rapidly changing areas:** `llms.txt` adoption, AI crawler policies, and the value of individual AI bot allowlists are still moving. Google currently says standard SEO requirements apply to AI Overviews/AI Mode, while OpenAI, Perplexity, and other AI systems expose separate crawler/user-fetcher controls. Treat `llms.txt` and AI-specific crawler monitoring as additive; do not let them distract from crawlable official pages, clean sitemaps, and structured property facts.
