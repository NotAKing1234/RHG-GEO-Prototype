## Q2: What Signals Do Dominant AI Engines Use to Surface Hotel Results?

### Dated Findings
**2025–2026** signal evidence:

- **Structured data (Schema.org):** Google and Microsoft publicly confirm use of Schema Markup for generative AI features. ChatGPT confirmed structured data determines product appearance. AI systems parse at scale and prioritize machine-readable context over visually appealing pages.
- **FAQ schema:** Single most impactful structured data type for AI citations in 2026. Pages with 5–8 well-implemented FAQ schema Q&A pairs cited up to 3x more than equivalent pages without FAQ schema.
- **Listicle / "Top N" format:** 74.2% of all AI citations come from structured "Top N" content. AI engines favor numbered, rankable lists.
- **Quick Answer blocks:** First 200 words must contain extractable ranked summaries. Above-the-fold placement strongly preferred.
- **Reviews and trust signals:** AI systems analyze community discussion and review sentiment to assess trust and increase recommendation probability.
- **Content freshness:** New content enters AI citation pools within 3–5 business days. Content decays without freshness updates.
- **Entity recognition:** Named brand + location + category + service type signals required for confident AI entity resolution.
- **Multi-modal content:** Google AI Overviews prefer YouTube and visual content (23.3% citation share).

## Q3: Current Best-Evidenced GEO and AEO Practices for Hospitality

### Dated Findings
**2025–2026** — Hospitality-specific GEO/AEO practices:

- **GEO defined:** Process of optimizing website content to be accurately understood, used, and prioritized by AI-driven search engines and AI agents. Goal is to become the primary recommendation within an AI-generated response, not just rank in a SERP.
- **AEO defined:** Optimizing content structure and topical authority to be the definitive response in AI-powered search. Key difference from SEO: zero-click answers are the target outcome.
- **Zero-click growth:** Zero-click searches rose from 56% (2024) to 69% (2025). AI Overviews appear in >30% of queries by late 2025.
- **Content structure requirements:**
  - Use FAQs, amenity descriptions, conversational language
  - Listicle-format ranking pages (74.2% AI citation rate)
  - Triple JSON-LD schema stacking: Article + ItemList + FAQPage on ranking pages
  - Quick Answer blocks in first 200 words
  - Scorecard comparison tables
- **Authority signals:** Reviews, consistent brand presence, third-party citations
- **Topical authority:** Comprehensive coverage of destination, amenity, and traveler-type content builds AI engine confidence in brand expertise
- **Content decay management:** Content must be refreshed to maintain citation pool presence

## Q5: Content Patterns for American Bleisure Travelers Using AI Search

### Dated Findings
**2025–2026** — Bleisure traveler AI search behavior:

- **Definition:** Bleisure = business + leisure; ages 25–55, US-based, traveling to Europe, increasingly using AI as primary hotel discovery interface
- **AI adoption rate:** 15% of Americans use AI for trip planning (Deloitte 2025, up from 10% in 2024); 83% have used or are interested in AI travel tools (2026 TravelBoom Leisure Travel Study)
- **Query evolution:** From generic ("best hotels in Paris") to highly specific, conversational queries ("I'm traveling to Paris in summer with two kids—what area and which hotel would you recommend?")
- **Bleisure-specific query patterns likely include:**
  - "hotel in [city] with meeting rooms and good WiFi for business travelers"
  - "best hotel in London near financial district for American executives"
  - "hotels with leisure amenities after business travel in Amsterdam"
  - "Radisson in [city] — is it good for American business travelers?"
  - "hotel with rooftop bar and spa near [European city center]"
- **Booking via AI:** 46% of AI travel users use it to explore accommodation; 42% have booked based on AI recommendations in 2025
- **Platform preferences:** ChatGPT for complex, multi-day scaffolding; Perplexity for research with citations; Google AI Overviews for quick comparisons

## Key Shifts Detected (vs. implicit prior assumptions)
1. **AI booking has arrived:** Perplexity and ChatGPT now support in-app hotel booking (2025). Visibility in AI = direct bookings, not just discovery.
2. **FAQ schema is the highest-ROI single action:** 3x citation boost documented. Previously viewed as "nice to have."
3. **OTAs are moving into AI ecosystems as distribution channels:** Tripadvisor/Perplexity partnership, Expedia/Copilot integration. Hotel brands without direct AI presence lose to OTAs even more severely than in traditional SEO.
4. **Content freshness now measurably decays in 3–5 day cycles:** This changes the content maintenance calculus significantly.
5. **Zero-click is now the majority:** 69% of Google searches result in zero clicks (2025). AI answer optimization is now the primary discovery battleground.
